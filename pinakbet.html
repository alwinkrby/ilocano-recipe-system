History & Cultural Significance

Pinakbet, also called pakbet, is a traditional Filipino vegetable dish that originated in the Ilocos region of the Philippines. The name comes from the Ilocano word "pinakebbet," meaning "shrunk" or "shriveled," referring to the cooking process where vegetables are reduced. Historically, this dish was born out of resourcefulness, utilizing locally available vegetables and bagoong (fermented fish paste), a staple in Ilocano cuisine. It reflects the agricultural heritage of the Ilocos region, where farming communities would cook whatever vegetables were in season. The dish has been passed down through generations and remains a cornerstone of Ilocano culinary identity.

Ingredients

  • 1/4 kg pork belly, cut into strips
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 cup squash, cubed
  • 1 cup okra
  • 1 cup bitter melon (ampalaya), sliced
  • 1 cup water
  • 2 tomatoes, quartered
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup eggplant, sliced
  • 1 cup string beans, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 3 tablespoons bagoong
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil

Step-by-Step Instructions

1
Prepare the Ingredients

Wash all vegetables thoroughly. Cut the pork belly into strips. Slice the eggplant, bitter melon, and onion. Quarter the tomatoes. Mince the garlic. Cut string beans into 2-inch pieces. Cube the squash into bite-sized pieces.

2
Sauté the Aromatics

Heat oil in a large pan or wok over medium heat. Sauté garlic until fragrant, then add onions and cook until translucent. Add tomatoes and cook until they soften and break down.

3
Cook the Pork

Add the pork belly strips to the pan and cook until they are lightly browned on all sides. This should take about 5-7 minutes.

4
Add Bagoong and Water

Pour in the bagoong (fermented fish paste) and mix well with the pork. Add water and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 10 minutes.

5
Layer the Vegetables

Start adding vegetables in layers, beginning with the ones that take longer to cook. First add squash, then eggplant, bitter melon, okra, and finally string beans on top. Do not stir.

6
Simmer Until Tender

Cover the pan and let the vegetables simmer for 10-15 minutes until tender but not mushy. The vegetables will cook in their own steam.

7
Mix and Serve

Once vegetables are cooked, gently mix everything together. Adjust seasoning if needed. Serve hot with steamed rice.
